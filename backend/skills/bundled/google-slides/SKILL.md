## Google Slides — Keyboard Shortcuts

Google Slides is a web-based presentation app at slides.google.com.

> Press `Ctrl + /` (Windows) or `⌘ + /` (macOS) to open the keyboard shortcuts help dialog inside Slides.
> Press `Alt + /` (Windows) or `Option + /` (macOS) to open Tool finder (search the menus).

### Tips

- **Present with `Ctrl + F5`** (Windows) or **`⌘ + Enter`** (macOS) — this starts from the current slide.
- **Black/white screen:** During a presentation, press `b` for black or `w` for white to pause visually.
- **Laser pointer** (`l` during presenting) gives a visual indicator for audiences.
- **Object manipulation:** `Tab` cycles through all objects on a slide — faster than clicking.
- Use `Ctrl + Alt + g` / `⌘ + Option + g` to group objects and manipulate them as one unit.
- On macOS, `⌘` replaces `Ctrl`, `Option` replaces `Alt`, and for Google Slides menus use `Ctrl + Option + letter`.
